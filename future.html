The Future of Text

When was the last time you kicked back, pulled out a copy of Nature, and had a casual morning read? Chances are, if you aren't Philip Campbell, the answer is never. The unfortunate reality of modern day texts is that for all the great discoveries and breakthroughs that impact billions of lives, the sheer amount of prerequisite knowledge necessary to comprehend academic, industrial, and judicial texts is beyond the reach of all but a fraction of a percent of the world's population. Compounded with the incessant demand for attention from email, social media, and other modern time drains, it is nearly impossible to simply focus and digest such texts.

Noted addresses this problem by providing a context-augmented interface for text that uses modern eye-tracking technologies to reveal supplementary information necessary to understand a text. Further, Noted provides a distraction-free environment for the reader by integrating a gesture controller that removes a user from the distractions accessible by keyboard and mouse - allowing a user to navigate the text and supplementary annotations with few, intuitive gestures. We hope you enjoy your experience with Noted, and welcome any feedback at aywang@ucsd.edu.
